    Things to Do

    Exciting Activities and Must-See Sights

    In Constanta

    Constanta offers a rich history, beautiful beaches, and various attractions to explore. Here are some things you can do in Constanta:

    • Visit the Old Town: Wander through the historic center to see ancient ruins, charming architecture, and vibrant streets filled with cafes, shops, and restaurants.
    • Explore the Constanta Casino: This iconic building is an architectural gem situated on the waterfront. While it's currently under renovation, it's worth admiring from the outside.
    • See the Roman Mosaics: Visit the Roman Edifice with Mosaic, where you can view well-preserved ancient Roman mosaics dating back to the 4th century AD.
    • Take a stroll on the promenade: The seafront promenade offers beautiful views of the Black Sea, lined with cafes, bars, and entertainment options.
    • Visit the Tomis Marina
    • Relax at Mamaia Beach: Just a short distance from Constanta, Mamaia Beach offers pristine sands and clear waters, making it perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and various water sports.
    • Experience nightlife in Mamaia: Enjoy the vibrant nightlife scene with numerous bars, clubs, and beach parties.

    In Dobrogea Region & Around

    Dobrogea, a historical region in southeastern Romania, is a diverse area known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural heritage. Here are some notable tourist attractions in Dobrogea.

    Around Constanta

    • 2 Mai and Vama Veche: Quaint seaside villages known for their bohemian atmosphere, lively beach bars, and alternative vibe, attracting artists, musicians, and free-spirited travelers.
    • Vadu Beach: A less crowded beach close to Constanta, Vadu offers a more natural and wild coastline, ideal for those seeking a quieter and unspoiled beach experience.
    • Tuzla Beach: A tranquil beach known for its therapeutic mud, Tuzla offers a relaxing atmosphere and the opportunity to experience natural mud treatments believed to have health benefits.
    • Techirghiol Lake: Known for its therapeutic mud and mineral-rich waters, Techirghiol Lake is a popular destination for spa treatments and relaxation.
    • Dobrogea Wine Region: Explore Dobrogea's emerging wine industry by visiting local vineyards and wineries, such as Murfatlar Winery, to taste delicious Romanian wines and learn about the region's winemaking traditions.

    Danube Delta

    • Danube Delta: One of Europe's most significant and biodiverse ecosystems, the Danube Delta is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore this unique wetland area by taking boat trips through its intricate network of waterways, discovering diverse flora and fauna, including numerous bird species.
    • Sulina: A picturesque town located at the Danube's mouth into the Black Sea, Sulina offers a relaxed atmosphere, sandy beaches, lighthouses, and a unique mix of cultures due to its historical role as a major port.
    • Gura Portitei Beach: Accessible by boat or ferry from Jurilovca, this remote and pristine beach on the shores of Razim Lake offers a peaceful escape, surrounded by unspoiled nature.
    • Letea Forest: Located within the Danube Delta Biosphere Reserve, this unique forest is known for its sand dunes, rare flora, wild horses, and water channels, offering an exceptional natural environment for exploration.

    Others

    • Macin Mountains: Explore Romania's oldest mountains, the Macin Mountains, characterized by unique rock formations, diverse flora, and hiking trails offering breathtaking views.
    • Dobrogea Gorges (Cheile Dobrogei): A geological reserve featuring picturesque gorges, cliffs, and caves, perfect for hiking and exploring nature. The area offers beautiful landscapes and unique rock formations.
    • Cape Caliacra (Kaliakra): While located in Bulgaria, Cape Caliacra is easily accessible from Constanta. It boasts stunning cliffs, ancient fortress ruins, and mesmerizing Black Sea views.
    • Balchik Palace and Botanical Garden (Bulgaria): About two hours drive from Constanta, Balchik Palace, also known as the Quiet Nest Palace, is a stunning complex with a botanical garden featuring a diverse collection of plants and beautiful architecture overlooking the Black Sea.

    Tips & Recommendations

    Unlocking Romania: Expert Tips & Local Insights

    Entry Requirements

    Passport and Visa

    Citizens from the EU, EEA, and several other countries enjoy visa-free travel to Romania for short stays. Verify specific visa regulations based on your nationality. 😀

    Cultural Tips

    Greetings

    Romanians generally greet with a handshake. For closer friends and family, cheek kisses (one or two, depending on the region) are common. Address people using their title (Mr., Mrs., Miss) followed by their surname until invited to use their first name.

    Punctuality

    Being on time is appreciated. If you're invited for a specific time, arriving within a 15-minute window is considered polite. While romanians are not known for their punctuality and there's a tendency for events to start a bit later than scheduled, for more formal occasions or events like weddings, being punctual is (very much 😀) appreciated.

    Language

    While many Romanians speak English, learning a few basic phrases in Romanian like "Mul»õumesc" (Thank you) or "BunƒÉ ziua" (Good day) can be appreciated and endearing.

    Respecting Customs

    Be respectful during any religious or traditional ceremonies.

    Gifts

    If you're planning to give gifts, presents are usually unwrapped when received. For weddings, cash is a common gift, presented in an envelope with a congratulatory note.

    Tipping

    Tipping around 10% in restaurants is customary if a service charge isn't included in the bill.

    Currency and Payments

    Currency Information

    Please note that the official currency of Romania is the Romanian Leu (plural: Lei), abbreviated as RON. The exchange rate is 1 RON = 0,20 Euro, but of course exchange rates fluctuate. Exchange money at banks or ATMs. Major credit cards are accepted in hotels and larger establishments, but cash might be necessary for smaller shops or markets.
